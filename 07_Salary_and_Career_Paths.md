# Salary and Career Paths: Official Sources

> Private employers generally do not publish standardized entry-level salary tables.
> This file keeps only official government sources and official JHU data.
> For private-sector salaries, use the platforms listed at the bottom to research current ranges.

---

## Official Federal Salary Data

### 2026 GS Pay Scale

Source: https://www.opm.gov/policy-data-oversight/pay-leave/salaries-wages/salary-tables/pdf/2026/GS.pdf

| Grade | Step 1 (Base) | Typical Entry Roles |
|-------|--------------|-------------------|
| GS-5 | $35,265 | Intern, entry clerk |
| GS-7 | $43,106 | Entry analyst with bachelor's degree |
| GS-9 | $52,727 | Analyst with master's degree or bachelor's + 1 year experience |
| GS-11 | $63,862 | Analyst with PhD or master's + 1 year experience |
| GS-12 | $76,553 | Mid-level analyst |
| GS-13 | $91,056 | Senior analyst / team lead |

These are base rates. Actual pay varies by locality. Use the official calculator:
https://www.opm.gov/policy-data-oversight/pay-leave/salaries-wages/2026/general-schedule-gs-salary-calculator/

## Where to Research Private-Sector Salaries
