## Official Johns Hopkins BME Facts

### Program Rankings

Source: https://www.bme.jhu.edu/about/why-hopkins-bme/

- Hopkins BME states that both its undergraduate and graduate programs are `consistently ranked #1 in the nation`

Source: https://www.bme.jhu.edu/news-events/news/hopkins-bme-graduate-programs-again-ranked-no-1-by-u-s-news/

- Hopkins BME graduate program was again ranked `No. 1` in the 2026 U.S. News release

### Career Outcomes

Source: https://www.bme.jhu.edu/academics/prospective-students/careers-in-bme/

- Undergraduate career destinations: `50%` graduate or medical school, `34%` industry, `6%` consulting, `4%` government, `6%` other
- Median annual salary for BME field: `more than $90,000`
- Job growth: `faster-than-average 10-year growth rate`
- Forbes ranking: `#1 major in terms of value`
- Past 5 years: Hopkins BME students generated millions in funding, launched `more than one dozen startup companies`, and won `hundreds of national awards`

### Research Exposure

Source: https://www.bme.jhu.edu/academics/

- `More than 95%` of BME students pursue research projects
- Johns Hopkins includes `3,000+` basic science and clinical laboratories

### Official Employer Partners

Source: https://www.bme.jhu.edu/academics/prospective-students/careers-in-bme/

Abbott Laboratories, Amazon, Baxter International, Becton Dickinson (BD), Boston Scientific, Bristol-Myers Squibb, Broad Institute of MIT and Harvard, Genentech, Google, IBM, Intuitive Surgical, Johnson & Johnson, Medtronic, National Institutes of Health (NIH), Personal Genome Diagnostics (PGDx), Pfizer, Philips, Samsung, Siemens Healthineers, Stryker, U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA)
